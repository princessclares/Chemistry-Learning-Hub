Discussion

Acids and bases are two fundamental categories of chemical substances. Acids typically have a sour taste and a pH less than 7. Common examples include vinegar (acetic acid) and lemon juice (citric acid). Bases, on the other hand, feel slippery, taste bitter, and have a pH greater than 7. Examples include baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) and soap.

The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14, where 7 is neutral. Acids donate hydrogen ions (H⁺) in a solution, while bases accept them or release hydroxide ions (OH⁻).

Understanding acids and bases is essential in chemistry because they participate in numerous chemical reactions, including neutralization reactions where an acid and base react to form water and a salt.
